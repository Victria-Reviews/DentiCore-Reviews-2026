Calcium
Enamel Fortification

Calcium is the primary mineral in tooth enamel and jawbone. Research suggests that adequate systemic calcium intake supports both enamel density and the alveolar bone that anchors teeth. Most adults in Tier One countries are mildly calcium deficient, making this a foundational inclusion in any oral health formula.

Iodine
Antimicrobial Oxygenator

Iodine has a well-documented history in dentistry as a broad-spectrum antimicrobial. Studies show it disrupts bacterial cell membranes and increases reactive oxygen species in gum tissue, creating an environment that is hostile to the anaerobic bacteria most commonly linked to periodontitis.

Copper
Collagen Synthesis

Copper is a required cofactor for lysyl oxidase, the enzyme that cross-links collagen fibers in connective tissue. Clinical evidence indicates that copper deficiency weakens gum attachment fibers, making gum recession more likely. Including copper addresses this specific structural vulnerability.

Chromium
Glucose Metabolism

Elevated blood sugar levels directly impair immune response in gum tissue and accelerate bacterial growth in the oral cavity. Chromium improves insulin sensitivity, which research links to reduced gingival inflammation in both diabetic and pre-diabetic individuals who experience disproportionate oral health challenges.

Chlorella Vulgaris
Detox and Biofilm Disruption

Chlorella is a green algae studied for its ability to bind and remove heavy metals and toxins from the digestive tract. Within the oral health context, research suggests it may help disrupt bacterial biofilm formation and reduce the adhesion of pathogenic bacteria to gum surfaces.

Boron
Bone and Gum Support

Boron plays an underrecognized role in bone mineralization and the regulation of inflammatory cytokines in gum tissue. Studies show that boron supplementation can reduce markers of gingival inflammation and support the hormonal environment needed for tissue repair.

Shilajit Extract
Adaptogenic Gum Repair

Shilajit is a mineral-rich resin from Himalayan rock formations containing fulvic acid and over 80 trace minerals. Clinical evidence indicates it has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties in oral mucosa, and some research suggests it may support faster healing of minor gum irritations.

Spearmint Leaf
Breath Freshening and Antimicrobial

Spearmint contains carvone, a natural compound with documented antimicrobial activity against common oral pathogens. Unlike synthetic flavoring agents, whole spearmint leaf provides both the sensory benefit of fresher breath and genuine antibacterial activity, particularly against Streptococcus mutans linked to cavity formation.
